Good morning.Here’s what you need to know:• Turkey bows over Kurds.Turkey shifted its position and will allow Iraqi Kurdish forces to cross its border with Syria to help fight militants besieging the city of Kobani.The change reflects escalating international pressure to push back Islamic State fighters.• Oscar Pistorius gets five years.The family of Reeva Steenkamp, slain by the South African track star on Valentine’s Day 2013, said “justice is served” by the sentence handed down today.The prosecution had sought a 10-year jail term.• Movement from Hong Kong’s government.Government leaders and pro-democracy demonstrators held talks today for the first time in three weeks of street protests.The city’s leader said earlier today that there was room for the committee that selects candidates for the territory’s 2017 election to be made “more democratic.”• Cluster weapons in Ukraine.Both the Ukrainian army and rebels appear to have used cluster munitions, which are banned in much of the world because of their possible widespread effect on civilians, in their fight for control over the eastern part of the country.• Dressmaker to the stars.Oscar de la Renta, whose luminous fashion career began in the 1950s in Franco’s Spain, died on Monday at his home in Kent, Conn. He was 82.• Stricter Ebola care.The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention issued voluntary but detailed guidelines for health care workers dealing with the Ebola virus after earlier criticism.The guidelines, based on the strict protocols used for years by Doctors Without Borders, might have prevented the infection of two nurses had they been in place a month ago.• Jet accident kills C.E.O.The chief executive of the French oil giant Total was killed when a business jet collided with a snow plow during takeoff in Moscow, where he traveled for a meeting with Prime Minister Dmitri A. Medvedev.Russian prosecutors said the snow plow driver was intoxicated, while other officials said snow limited visibility.• Sports betting challenge.The four major professional sports leagues and the National Collegiate Athletic Association are filing suit today to halt New Jersey’s legalized sports betting, which is scheduled to start Sunday.Gov. Chris Christie has been leading the state’s efforts to circumvent a 1992 federal law that banned the sanctioning or licensing of sports betting.MARKETS• Wall Street stocks are sharply higher.
